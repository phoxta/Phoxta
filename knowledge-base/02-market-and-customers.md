# 02 — Market & Customers

## The problem (three populations)
1. **The would-be founder** — has an idea/audience but not 6 months or $30k to assemble a stack. Pains: decision fatigue (Shopify vs Wix vs Webflow), integration tax, costly wrong-stack mistakes, months to revenue, manual ops, AI is hard to bolt on.
2. **The operator-buyer** — capable, has capital/time/audience; wants to acquire a working business. Pains: no unit-economics transparency, inherited legacy debt, migration risk, absent/brittle AI, owner-concentration risk, poor matching.
3. **The investor / capital allocator** — small-business PE, search funders, family offices, investor groups. Pains: diligence cost exceeds sub-$500k deals, opaque tech quality, can't easily group-invest, hard to find aligned operators, poor exit liquidity, carry/fees don't work at small size.

**Unifying problem:** there is no system where the people who can build, run, and fund businesses are in the same room with verified, AI-native, working products in between. *Phoxta is that room.*

## Jobs-to-be-Done (6 core jobs)
1. "Help me launch a real business in weeks" — success: time-to-first-revenue <30 days.
2. "Help me acquire a business that won't break" — success: close <60 days, no post-close surprises.
3. "Help me find people to build/run my business with" — success: ≥1 productive match/quarter.
4. "Help me run my business with fewer people" — success: ops hours/week reduced 50%+.
5. "Help me invest in a productive small business" — success: 15%+ IRR over 5 years.
6. "Help me earn a living by running businesses for others" — success: equity + recurring income within 90 days.
