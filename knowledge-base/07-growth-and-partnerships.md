# 07 — Growth & Partnerships

## Growth strategy — loops over budgets
> "Marketing buys growth. Loops compound it. We design the platform around loops first, then spend marketing where loops need ignition." — *Growth Charter*

**Five growth loops:**
1. **Blueprint → buyer** — every listed business is a marketing artifact (subtle "Powered by Phoxta"). ~1.3–1.6× organic lift per active blueprint.
2. **Customer → story → customer** — success stories drive new buyers (5–15 buyers per well-told story).
3. **Author → blueprint → buyer → author** — buyers become authors (30%+ of catalog from 3rd parties by Yr4).
4. **Matching → trust → matching** — each match boosts reputation + model (matching participation 2×/yr from Yr2).
5. **AI → savings → adoption** — gateway optimizations lower cost → more usage → lower churn (~10% NRR uplift/yr).

## International expansion (sequenced, not opportunistic)
- **Phase 1 (Yr1–2) English-speaking core:** UK (HQ), Ireland, US, Canada, Australia, NZ, Singapore (~75% of addressable market). Yr-2 customer targets: US 180, UK 150, Canada 30, Australia 20, Ireland 10, Singapore 10.
- **Phase 2 (Yr3–4):** Western Europe (Spain, Germany, France, Netherlands) + India.
- **Phase 3 (Yr4–5+):** LatAm, Middle East, SE Asia (Brazil, Mexico, UAE, Saudi, Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam).

**Localization cost/market:** currency+tax $30–80k · language UX $50–100k/lang · content $80–150k/lang/yr · legal entity $50–200k · payments $10–30k · support $80k+ · marketing $100–300k/market/yr.

**Readiness checklist (per market):** 200+ organic visitors · launch partner · legal review · tax/payment infra · first operating customer · localized site · local-hours support · local-currency pricing tested.

**Anti-patterns:** many markets at once · translating copy not product · markets without expertise/partners · treating an operating problem as a marketing problem · hiring a country GM before the market is real.

## Partnerships & ecosystem
**Philosophy:** partnerships for technology leverage, distribution acceleration, ecosystem extension. "Partnerships are a strategic lever, not a fundraising signal."

- **Technology (Tier 1):** Anthropic (Claude — primary LLM), Stripe (payments + Connect), Vercel (Next.js hosting), Supabase/Postgres (DB + RLS). **Tier 2:** Twilio/SendGrid/Resend/OneSignal, Cloudflare (CDN/R2/WAF), Algolia/Typesense, Mux/Cloudinary, Segment/RudderStack.
- **Distribution — reseller agencies (Yr2+):** resell blueprints; **20% recurring on platform fees + 15% on clone fees for 24 months**; co-branded materials, agency-tier console.
- **Founder communities:** Indie Hackers, On Deck, YC Startup School, Antler/EF/Sequoia Arc, StartupGrind.
- **Investor networks:** Search Fund Accelerators, AngelList syndicates, family offices, micro-funds (Calm Fund, Tiny Capital, Permanent Equity).
- **Ecosystem extension:** Blueprint author program (Yr2+, authors earn **60% of clone fees**, Phoxta 20% rev-share on platform fees); operator network; education/certification (Yr3+).

**Partner program metrics (Yr2 → Yr5):** reseller agencies 10→100 · blueprint authors 5→150 · operators 20→500 · % revenue via partners 10%→40%.

## Strategic alliances (shape the game)
Distinct from commercial partnerships — they shape rules/regulation/standards. Domains: (1) Small-business advocacy (FSB, NFIB, etc.); (2) Multi-tenant SaaS architecture community; (3) AI ethics & safety (gateway gives governance primitives); (4) Acquisition-entrepreneurship community (Stanford SFP, INSEAD, IESE); (5) University partnerships (LBS, Stanford GSB, Wharton, INSEAD, IIM Bangalore). Owned at CEO/Head-of-Policy level; annual go/no-go review; funded mostly by founder time.
